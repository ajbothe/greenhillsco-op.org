Enhancing Education Through Parental Cooperation for over 50 years

Greenhills Cooperative Preschool provides a unique opportunity for parents to work with their child, their child's teacher, and friends as they together begin to explore new activities and materials. Our state-licensed facility is owned, maintained, and administered by parents. Small classes are taught by experienced, qualified teachers.

Our two classrooms and indoor play area are downstairs at Greenhills Community Church - Presbyterian. The Co-op is nondenominational, not-for-profit and state licensed. We welcome families of diverse religious beliefs.

Our Mission: To provide preschoolers a safe and caring atmosphere where they can interact with other preschoolers and learn with the guidance of their teachers and parents. To provide parents the opportunity to be an integral part of their child's early learning experiences. To provide the community with a preschool committed to helping children develop their social, emotional, physical, and intellectual potential.

Typical classroom schedule includes:

  • Choice Time (Artwork, Cooking, Theme Play)
  • Clean-UpCircle (Music, Show & Tell, etc.)
  • Restroom and Snack
  • Story Time, Special Helper's Show & Tell, and Music
  • Large Motor Skills / Playground
  • Dismissal

Time allotted for these activities will change during the course of the year as your child matures.

  
Special Features:

Field Trips: Cincinnati Zoo, Cincinnati Museum of Natural History, Parky’s Farm, Greenhills Post Office, Winton Woods, Greenhills Fire Department, Bigg’s, and Greenhills Library have hosted previous trips.

Visits: Doctors, fire fighters, police officers, librarians, nurses, dentists and veterinarians have visited our classes in the past and taught children important lessons about safety, hygiene, animals, careers and community services.

Animals: Pets in each classroom, such as fish, guinea pigs, hamsters and frogs give children a chance to learn and care for other living things.

Playground: Features include large and small slides, climbing equipment and a tire swing. A large secluded patio next to the playground gives the children a place to ride big wheels and play with hula-hoops, sidewalk chalk, etc.

Parties: Halloween, Thanksgiving, Christmas, Valentine’s Day and Easter are celebrated and Hanukkah is discussed. We have Pumpkin Night in October, Family Night in January, Bunny Brunch near Easter, Mothers’ Day in May, a School Picnic at the end of the year and we march in the Greenhills Pioneer Days Parade
